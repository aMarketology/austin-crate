## Why 10K Impressions Is the Right Target

Impressions measure how many times our pages appear in Google search results, regardless of click. Getting to 10K/week means:

- Google is **finding, trusting, and surfacing** our pages for real queries
- We have enough **topical coverage** that Google considers us an authority on Austin specialty shipping
- Clicks (and leads) follow naturally once impressions compound

**The math:**
```
10,000 impressions/week
x 5% avg CTR
= 500 clicks/week (2,000+/month)
x 3% conversion rate
= 60 leads/month
```

Even at half that CTR (2.5%), we are at 30+ leads/month. Impressions are the engine. Everything below drives impressions.

## The 3 Levers to 10K Impressions

### Lever 1: Page Volume (Most Important)
**Every new indexed page = new keyword opportunities = more impressions.**
Going from 27 to 70+ pages is the single biggest driver.

### Lever 2: Keyword Targeting
**Each page must target a specific, real search query.**
Generic pages rank for nothing. Specific pages rank for something.

### Lever 3: Internal Link Authority
**Every new page must be linked from at least 2 existing pages.**
Google discovers and trusts pages through links. Orphaned pages do not rank.

> **Why the ramp is slow early:** Google takes 2-8 weeks to index new pages and 2-4 months for newer domains to see competitive rankings. Pages published in April start generating real impressions in June. Pages published in June peak in August-September. This is normal -- the ramp accelerates because older pages keep improving while new pages are added.
